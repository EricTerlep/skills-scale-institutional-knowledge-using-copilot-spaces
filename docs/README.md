# OctoAcme Project Management Docs

Welcome to the OctoAcme project management documentation hub. This README serves as the entry point for understanding OctoAcme's key project management processes, navigating the process documents, and onboarding new team members quickly.

## Overview

OctoAcme's project management processes are structured to ensure clarity, continuity, and high-quality delivery for all cross-functional initiatives. At the core, projects flow through a clear lifecycle beginning with initiation (problem scoping and stakeholder alignment), followed by collaborative planning, disciplined execution and tracking, culminating in structured release, deployment, and retrospective learning. This lifecycle is underpinned by standardized key artifacts such as the Project One-pager, roadmaps, sprint backlogs, risk registers, and robust definitions of done—all of which are maintained within the project repository for transparency and accessibility.

The core roles and personas in this process are intentionally defined to promote clear ownership and collaboration. Product Managers establish the vision, prioritize the backlog, and specify measurable outcomes, while Project Managers handle scheduling, risk management, inter-team coordination, and reporting. Developers are responsible for implementing features to agreed standards and maintaining quality through tests and reviews. Specialized roles—including QA Lead, Business Analyst, UX/UI Designer, Scrum Master, and Security Champion—each bring focused expertise that reduces hand-off friction, closes accountability gaps, and strengthens quality across every phase of delivery.

Central to OctoAcme's workflow is a rhythm of cadenced communication and a rigorous approach to quality. Regular standups, weekly syncs, and milestone reviews keep teams aligned, with project boards in GitHub visually tracking work through states such as Backlog, In Progress, Review, QA, and Done. Work is broken down into small, testable increments, and each pull request is expected to reference acceptance criteria and pass automated linting and tests before review. Quality assurance is multi-layered, combining automated CI pipelines, unit and integration tests, manual acceptance as needed, and continuous monitoring of velocity and risk.

Finally, OctoAcme emphasizes risk management and learning as continuous threads throughout delivery. Risks are proactively captured, assessed, and monitored via transparent registers, with escalation paths clearly mapped for blockers or incidents. After releases or major events, teams conduct retrospectives to identify what worked and what could be improved, translating learnings into actionable backlog items. This fosters a culture of incremental improvement, where process clarity, measurable results, and team feedback drive ongoing enhancement of project management practices.
